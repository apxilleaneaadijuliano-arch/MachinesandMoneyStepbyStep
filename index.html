Step 1: Group Discussion

Our group revisited the problem from our previous RCA: the high cost of machines. We brainstormed several possible solutions, such as sharing equipment, repairing old machines, or finding cheaper alternatives. However, most of these ideas had limitations. After weighing the pros and cons, we realized that focusing on multi-use food machines could directly address the issue.

Step 2: Research

We researched common household food machines and explored how they could be adapted for multiple uses:

  • Blender – smoothies, soups, grinding spices, making paper pulp
  • Rice Cooker – rice, steaming, slow-cooking, baking simple cakes
  • Oven – baking, roasting, dehydrating fruits, sterilizing jars
  • Mixer – dough, whipping cream, grinding meat, pasta making
  • Juicer – juice, nut milk, natural dyes
  • Grill – cooking meat, sandwiches, frying eggs, roasting beans
  • Food Processor – chopping, kneading, shredding, making spreads

Step 3: Concluding Ideas

From our research, we concluded that multi-use machines are practical because they:

  • Reduce the need to buy separate appliances (lowering costs)
  • Save space in kitchens or classrooms
  • Encourage sustainability by extending the usefulness of existing machines
